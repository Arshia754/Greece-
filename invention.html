Olympics

Today, the Olympic Games are a global phenomenon. The first record of the Olympic Games being held was in 776 B.C. when a cook named Coroebus won the only event a 630-feet footrace called the stade to become Olympic champion. However, it is likely that the games had been going on for many years by that time. They were held every four years in Olympia, on the western Peloponnese peninsula. The first modern Olympics took place in Athens, Greece. There were 280 participants from 13 nations who competed in 43 events including track and field, gymnastics, swimming, wrestling, cycling, tennis, weightlifting, shooting, and fencing.

Theatre

An early form of drama emerged in Greece around the 6th century B.C. Although theater in India may have claimed this, it is the Greek version that has influenced the later development of theater in the West. Greek writers also created plays in multiple genres, including tragedy, comedy and many of the plays they wrote are still relevant today and have helped to shape modern western culture. The ancient Greeks also constructed theaters capable of seating hundreds or thousands of people.

Water Clock

The water clock known as Clepsydra was created in ancient Greece around 325 BC. It is a timepiece that measures time by the regulated flow of liquid into or out of a vessel. The liquid is measured by the time. Water clocks are ancient and are known to have existed in Babylon, Egypt and Persia around the 16th century B.C. However, the Greeks made this technology and used it to measure a huge amount of events. It was used in trials, where the time of the lawyers and the witnesses speeches were measured to make sure that everyone spoke for the same amount of time.

The Lever

The earliest evidence of a lever goes back to the ancient Near East around 5000 B.C. They were used in ancient India and Egypt to lift water and to move heavy objects. Some of the earliest remaining pieces about levers are from the 3rd century B.C. and were written by the Greek polymath Archimedes. It was Archimedes who first explained the ratios of force, load and distance.

Cartography

Cartography is the study and practice of making maps. It has played an important role in travel and navigation since ancient times. The earliest evidence of cartography is ancient Babylon however, it was the Greeks who brought cartography into the world and discovered its possibilities. Anaximander was one of the cartographers to create a world map. He was born between 611-610 BCE and he created map of the ancient world ,and made important contributions to astronomy and geography.
